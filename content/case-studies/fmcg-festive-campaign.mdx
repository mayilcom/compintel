## Background

A mid-size biscuits brand with three hero SKUs and a presence across general trade and quick commerce. Their primary competitors: two large national FMCG brands with significantly larger media budgets.

The challenge: knowing when to move. With a smaller budget, they couldn't match competitor spend — but they could move faster if they saw campaigns coming early.

## What happened

In late August, the brand's Sunday brief flagged an unusual Instagram activity pattern for their primary competitor. Post frequency had jumped from 4 posts/week to 11 posts/week over 8 days. The creative theme had shifted — NutriChoice packaging prominently featured, lifestyle imagery with family occasions.

The signal type was **Watch**.

Seven days later, the brief flagged new Meta ad creatives: 14 new ads with festive packaging creative, all launched on September 3. The signal upgraded to **Threat**.

The brand's CMO had seen the Watch signal the week before and had already briefed the agency. By the time the Threat signal arrived, creative was in review.

## The response

The brand pulled their planned Diwali campaign forward by two weeks. Their gifting pack hit quick commerce the same week as the competitor's campaign — instead of arriving two weeks after.

## The outcome

Category share on quick commerce held through the festive period. The CMO estimated the early launch generated approximately 15% more gifting pack sales than the previous year's reactive approach.
